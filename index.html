Grant Imahara

Here is a timeline of Imahara's life:

  • 1970: Grant Imahara is born in Los Angeles, California.
  • 1988-1993: Imahara attends the University of Southern California, where he earns a Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering.
  • 1993-1999: Grant Imahara starts his career at Lucasfilm's THX division, where he worked as an engineer.
  • 1999-2005: Imahara transitions to the world of special effects and joins Industrial Light & Magic (ILM), a division of Lucasfilm. During this time, he contributes to movies such as "The Matrix Reloaded," "The Matrix Revolutions," and "Star Wars: Episode I - The Phantom Menace."
  • 2005-2014: Grant Imahara gains widespread recognition as one of the hosts of the popular television show "MythBusters." His engineering expertise and charismatic on-screen presence make him a fan favorite.
  • 2010: Imahara participates in "The Guild" web series, showcasing his acting skills.
  • 2014-2015: After leaving "MythBusters," Imahara continues to pursue various projects. He co-hosts the Netflix series "White Rabbit Project," where he and his co-hosts investigate scientific and technological topics.
  • 2016-2017: Grant Imahara takes part in the web series "Star Trek Continues," playing the character of Lt. Sulu.
  • 2019: Imahara hosts and produces the web series "Circuit Breaker," exploring various aspects of engineering and technology.
  • 2020: Grant Imahara passes away on July 13, 2020, at the age of 49. His death is attributed to a brain aneurysm.
